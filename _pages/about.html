We are an urban policy startup producing practical research.

Our mission is to tackle pressing problems facing midsize American cities using Chattanooga, Tennessee, as a living lab to pilot creative policy ideas, conduct sound research and provide practical recommendations.

Our team is made up of academics, policymakers and journalists. We believe creative thinking driven by objective research is the best way to make real progress on today’s most pressing challenges faced by communities across  the country.

Big ideas for small cities

There are over 400 American cities with populations between 75,000 and 500,000 residents, but there are few resources to help these communities tackle the big policy challenges they face. We aim to meet this need by providing smart ideas and sound research to strengthen the nation’s midsize cities.

Our goals

Foster informed discussions

We believe that research written only for a small group of academics or elected officials isn’t the most productive way to create a better society.

Develop innovative solutions

We are committed to developing out-of-the-box policy prescriptions that rethink the way we see the challenges in our communities.

Support local governments

The work that takes place in city halls is more important than ever, so we aim to be a key resource for leaders in communities and local governments across the country.

Outcomes over output

We don’t measure our success by the volume of research we publish. Data and analysis alone won’t lead to better outcomes. We aim for our findings to engage and empower communities to develop actionable plans for change.

We’re committed to being an accessible resource for local communities

Metro Ideas Project is a nonpartisan, independent public policy research organization that strives to identify and address the pressing issues facing American cities by conducting sound research, sparking informed policy discussion and providing practical recommendations.

The ideas, debates and proposals that are sparked by good policy research are key to building better cities. Today, research can’t just be locked away in an inaccessible and lengthy document with the expectation that its conclusions will trickle down to policymakers and the public. To foster the next generation of engaged civic actors, we strive to design research projects that connect to our lives in a way that’s meaningful and compelling.

Our research agenda is driven by five key factors

To make the most of our resources, we determine the policy focus of each research project through a careful review of some core qualities. There are several factors that help us find policy challenges and research opportunities that we can add value to our work:

  1. Local impact
  2. Accessibility
  3. Data availability
  4. Engaged stakeholders
  5. Relevance

Additionally, we look for policy areas that we are personally passionate about as a team and see if there is an opportunity for further study. That creative spark combined with qualitative data from community leaders is what determines the particular policy areas we choose to focus on.

We are committed to intellectually honest and rigorous scholarship

Our team is empowered to take positions and make recommendations shaped by research findings.

Diversity in thought, experience and ideology are core to our mission of building a new kind of research organization that values the best ideas over rigid political agendas. The Metro Ideas Project's financial supporters have no influence over any aspect of research project design, analysis, findings or recommendations.

We are a 501(c)(3) nonpartisan organization that neither makes endorsements nor engages in electioneering.

Metro Ideas Project closed a "philanthropic seed round" in October 2015. The partners at Lamp Post Group provided a grant to launch and fund the organization for two years.

Additional funding will expand our capabilities and provide the opportunity for broader impact. All future contributions will be publicly acknowledged and listed online. The Metro Ideas Project will consider granting anonymity to individual funders who request it, subject to an internal review that determines the best interests of the organization and the funder with regard to integrity, our credibility and the law.

Creative thinking drives our policy

We are an independent, nonprofit research startup devoted to the analysis and design of public policies that help make midsize cities work better for people. Our team is made up of academics, policymakers and journalists. We believe creative thinking driven by objective research is the best way to make real progress on today’s most pressing challenges faced by communities across the country.

We're a multidisciplinary team of doers.

We are a diverse team of talented thinkers, passionate and knowledgeable about a wide range of urban issues. Our staff is assembled from the ranks of tech, academia, journalism, and creative agencies.

It’s our priority to reach beyond the typical mix of talent found in traditional institutions in order to build an organization capable of tackling public sector challenges in the 21st century.
